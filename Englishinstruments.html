The Old English Folk Instruments

Here are the most well known instruments played in the English tradition, gleaned from Twenty-two musical instruments of England. Besides the usual way of categorising musical instruments - string, woodwind, brass and percussion - have you ever wondered if there exist other ways of classifying them? As it so happens, you'd be spot on! The film cited here above, describes four families of instruments that I had never heard of till a couple of days back. They are the chordophones, the idiophones, the membranophones and the aerophones.

The family of Chordophones


  • Banjo

  • Cittern, Cithren or English guitar

  • Crwth

  • Fiddle

  • Giga

  • Hammered Dulcimer

  • Hurdy-Gurdy

  • Lyre

The family of Idiophones


  • Keyboard Glockenspiel

The family of Membranophones


  • Crowdy-Crawn

  • Tabor

The family of Aerophones


  • Birch Trumpet
  • English Bagpipes
  • English Flageolet
  • English Flute
  • Euphonium
  • Low Whistle
  • Tin Whistle
  • Tremolo Harmonica
